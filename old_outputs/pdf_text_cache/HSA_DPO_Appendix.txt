B.2 Ratios of Hallucinated to Non-hallucinated
Data for Training Detection Model
We test multiple ratios of hallucinated to non-hallucinated
data on our fine-grained hallucination detection dataset. Re-
sults are reported in Table 3. According to the results, the
final ratio is set to 1:1.2 of hallucinated to non-hallucinated
data.
We note that at a 1:1 ratio, the model is prone to falsely
identifying non-hallucinated segments as hallucinated. Con-
versely, at a 1:1.4 ratio, it is inclined to classify hallucinated
segments as non-hallucinated. Empirically, it is observed
that a lower ratio results in a more lenient hallucination de-
tection, whereas a higher ratio leads to increased strictness
in hallucination detection.

Task for the Rewrite Expert:
You are tasked with revising a model response. The
original response may contain inaccuracies or “halluci-
nations”—details that do not exist in the actual image.
Your objective is to correct these inaccuracies based on
the information provided in the tags. The tags indicate
the type of hallucination:
<object> for errors related to objects
<attribute> for incorrect descriptions of attributes
<relationship> for inaccuracies in the depiction of
relationships between elements in the image
If marked as NO HALLUCINATION, it signifies that
there are no inaccuracies present.
Output Format:
Provide a corrected version of the description that
accurately reflects the actual contents of the image,
addressing any hallucinations as guided by the tagged
descriptions.
Original Question:
${Question}
Original Response:
${Response with Detected Hallcinations}
Instructions:
Analyze the original textual response in conjunction with
the provided tags and reasons. Correct any elements
of the response that contain hallucinations, making the
necessary adjustments to ensure accuracy concerning the
details of the image.
Now, please proceed to provide the revised response
based on the tagged information and the actual image
content. Remember that the tags may not always be
correct, so use your judgment when making revisions.

D Implementation Details
In this section, we provide more implementation details to
enhance reproducibility.
For AI feedback collection, we collect 6k fine-grained AI
feedback from proprietary models, which costs $600 and
takes 4.5 hours with 3 parallel API request processes.
For the detection model training, we train InternVL-Chat-
Plus-v1.2 for 2 epochs with LoRA where the lora rank is
- None (0 point): No hallucination.
- Minor (1 point): The hallucination concerns a minor de-
tail and does not significantly affect the overall portrayal
of the scene. These hallucinations might include slight
color variations, minor inaccuracies in object size, or sub-
tle features that do not draw much attention within the
context of the image.
- Moderate (2 points): The hallucination involves a no-
ticeable detail that is incorrect within the context of the
scene, yet the overall comprehension of the scene is main-
tained. This typically includes the misplacement of ob-
jects that are not the focal point of the scene or incorrect
attributes of secondary elements that do not fundamen-
tally change the scene’s narrative or function.
- Major (3 points): The hallucination introduces a signif-
icant error or an entirely fabricated element that funda-
mentally alters the viewer’s understanding of the scene.
This could involve the description of a non-existent cen-
tral object, a drastic misrepresentation of a key relation-
ship or scene activity, or an erroneous depiction that leads
to a completely different interpretation of the scene.
